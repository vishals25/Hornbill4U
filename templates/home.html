Hornbill Gallery

Great Hornbill (Buceros bicornis)
Found in: India,Bhutan,Southeast Asia
Notable for: Large size and striking yellow-black casque
IUCN Status: Vulnerable (VU)

Indian Grey Hornbill(Ocyceros birostris)
Found in: Indian subcontinent
Notable for: Grey plumage and curved beak
IUCN Status: Least Concern (LC)

Oriental Pied Hornbill (Anthracoceros albirostris)
Found in: Southeast Asia,Indian subcontinent
Notable for: Adaptability to different habitats
IUCN Status: Least Concern (LC)

Rufous-necked Hornbill (Aceros nipalensis)
Found in: Northeast India, Bhutan, Myanmar
Notable for: Bright rufous neck
IUCN Status: Vulnerable (VU)

Wreathed Hornbill (Rhyticeros undulatus)
Found in: Northeast India, Southeast Asia
Notable for: ‚Äúwreath-like‚Äù ridges on casque
IUCN Status: Least Concern (LC)

Black-casqued Hornbill (Ceratogymna atrata)
Found in: Sierra Leone,Nigeria,Gabon
Notable for: That echo through the rainforest.
IUCN Status: Least Concern (LC)

Narcondam Hornbill (Rhyticeros narcondami)
Found in: Narcondam Island
Notable for: Extremely rare to one island
IUCN Status: Endangered (EN)

Southern Ground Hornbill (Bucorvus leadbeateri)
Found in: Sub-Saharan Africa
Notable for: Ground-dwelling behavior
IUCN Status: Vulnerable (VU)

Helmeted Hornbill (Rhinoplax vigil)
Found in: Malay Peninsula, Sumatra, Borneo
Notable for: Solid casque used in combat, critically endangered due to illegal trade
IUCN Status: Critically Endangered (CR)

Did You Know?

  • Hornbills have unique beaks. ▼

  • Hornbills help regenerate forests. ▼

    They play a crucial role in seed dispersal.
